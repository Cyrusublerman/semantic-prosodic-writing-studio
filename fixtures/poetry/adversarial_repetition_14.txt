The wind the wind the wind along the path,
The wind turns grass to gold and turns again,
Again the wind the wind the wind the rain,
Rain rain rain rain upon the meadow grass.

I walk I walk I walk the broken metre here
With five then twelve then three then twenty feet,
No rhyme no reason holding any line
Except the wind the wind the wind repeat.

Quiet river? No — the wind the wind instead,
Leaves leaves leaves leave the luminous dream dead,
And earth remembers nothing but the wind,
The wind the wind until the poem ends.

Keep this last couplet short for diagnosis,
Wind wind wind — the fault is repetition.
