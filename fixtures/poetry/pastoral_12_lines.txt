Along the quiet river bend the willows lean,
And meadow grass remembers every silver rain,
While aureate light returns across the green
And hush of dusk still holds the day's refrain.

A zephyr lifts the leaves in soft array,
The earth keeps chronicles of seed and stone,
And every track the cattle wore by day
Becomes a path where evening walks alone.

No trumpet calls; the pastoral hour is still,
Yet luminous the rivulet's low song,
And in that cadence field and distant hill
Keep metre with the dark that will not long.
